About Me

I am an Electrical and Robotics Engineering student at the California Institute of Technology, deeply interested in swarm robotics, control theory, reinforcement learning, and mechatronics. My passion lies in designing and interfacing robotic systems, with a particular focus on mechanical and electrical components as well as motion planning algorithms.

Currently, I am engaged in cutting-edge research at Caltech's Autonomous Robotics and Control Lab, where I'm developing systems for multi-quadcopter coordination and implementing advanced filtering and machine learning techniques for real-time trajectory optimization.

My notable projects include the development of a virtual dual-arm ball-setting robot, color-based object detection and pick-and-place systems, and the design of chassis and arm autonomy systems for lunar modular rovers. I am also a recipient of the prestigious Korean Presidential Science Scholarship.
